The Search for Animorphs

Five teens acquire the ability to morph into any animal they touch and are swept into an endless alien war. To read about them old-school style (aka borrowing the books from libraries), I learned that you'd have to go on a little adventure yourself.

By Florina Sutanto



Earlier this summer, as rabbits darted up and down the park near my sublet and red cardinals, unafraid of the humans they soared above, pecked at nuts and berries people tossed at them during their lunch breaks, I started thinking about Animorphs again. In 7th grade, I devoured the series with an intensity only reserved for the most seminal books of my childhood. I watched the short-lived TV adaptation under the covers in my grandmother's spare room, and kept my class's copy of The Invasion (Book #1) at home for an entire year because I really liked the lenticular cover. The live-action morphs were nowhere near as scary as what a 13 year old's mind could conjure in the dark. And those descriptions were graphic. Source.

You can never really outgrow books, is what I've learned since then. Animorphs, with its deceptively bizzare covers that feature each phase of a character morphing into an animal (or an alien, sometimes), was written by K.A. Applegate, her husband Michael Grant, and a team of ghostwriters. The books sold over 35 million copies altogether but fizzled out

In 2011, Scholastic relaunched the series with lenticular covers to replace the uncanny yet charming art on the original books (all created by artist David B. Mattingly) and updated cultural references here and there in hopes of drawing in 'a whole new generation of readers'. The project fizzled out after 7-8 books and was cancelled due to low sales. There's no shortage of libraries here. The Tisch Library at Tufts is only a 5 minute walk away in one direction. The Somerville Public Library West Branch, which I am a card-carrying member of, is an 8 minute walk in the other.

I didn't have strong expectations for the former, but I was surprised to learn that the children's section of the public library didn't have any of the Animorphs books.
